Background

With the rapid development in the field of machine manufacturing and maintenance, intelligence methods have received widespread attention and have been studied deeply. To keep track of the hot research topics and promote academic exchanges in related fields, the Mechanical Intelligent Automation Conference (MIA) will be held on Nov 26th at Chongqing Grand Marriott Hotel. The MIA is organized and sponsored by the College of Mechanical and Transportation Engineering, Chongqing University, and some renowned scholars will attend and listen to reports. The conference mainly focuses on the application of intelligence methods in machine manufacturing and maintenance, aiming to provide an academic platform for the exchange of relevant research advances and experience. Main topics include but are not limited to (1) Machining and manufacturing, (2) Machine Component Intelligence Maintenance, (3) Intelligence Robots, (4) Equipment health management, etc

Research on the hybrid modeling method of complex rotor systems coupled with flexible foundation transfer function characteristic data

This presentation covers two distinct methods: the Experimental Modal Analysis and System Identification techniques employed to derive a reduced-order model of a flexible foundation from measured frequency response function data. Additionally, it delves into the Rotor-Flexible Foundation System Modeling and Computational Methods aimed at obtaining time-domain solutions for the proposed hybrid model.

  • Start: 10.30 AM
  • End: 10.45 AM
  • Location: Houde room 514 (5F), 8th Building, CQU

Improved tuning control for semi-active seat suspension

This research developed a novel tuning control strategy for magnetorheological (MR) seat suspension, resulting in a 27% reduction in vibration levels and successful avoidance of end-stop impacts compared to passive seats. The study involved testing MR damper characteristics, establishing a mathematical model, designing an improved control strategy, and verifying its effectiveness through experiments.

  • Start: 10.45 AM
  • End: 11.00 AM
  • Location: Houde room 514 (5F), 8th Building, CQU

Heuristic method of adaptive filtering for noisy GARCH processes

In this programme, the filtering problems of the discrete systems with noisy GARCH(1,1) processes are discussed by the heuristic method. Based on the intuition that the density function of the noisy GARCH(1,1) process can be well approximated by its driving noise density function with time-varying variance, three different approximating methods are proposed, respectively.

  • Start: 11.00 AM
  • End: 11.15 AM
  • Location: Houde room 514 (5F), 8th Building, CQU

SCADA data-driven attention mechanism networks for detecting blade icing states of wind turbines

To cope with the class imbalance and cross-turbine data distribution discrepancy of SCADA datasets, the blade icing detection methods based on SCADA data and attention mechanism networks are proposed to identify the blade state in single-turbine and cross-turbine research scenarios. A self-adaptive weight loss function and transfer learning algorithm are integrated into the network.

  • Start: 11.25 AM
  • End: 11.40 AM
  • Location: Houde room 514 (5F), 8th Building, CQU

Dynamic Characteristics and Optimization of Segmented Fork Arm Seat System

To improve the low-frequency vibration isolation performance and driving comfort of the engineering seat system, a segmented fork arm seat system is proposed. The equivalent model of the n-level segmented seat system is established, the amplitude-frequency response function and phase-frequency response function of the segmented seat system are solved and analyzed. According to the actual application condition of a loader, the engineering structure of the 3-level segmented fork arm seat system was built, and the structure-stiffness parameters of the segmented fork arm seat system were optimized by multi-objective genetic algorithm.

  • Start: 11.40 AM
  • End: 11.55 AM
  • Location: Houde room 514 (5F), 8th Building, CQU

Research on Kinematics and Path Planning Problems of Tea Picking Robotic Arm

This presentation optimized tea-picking robotic arm kinematics with an enhanced algorithm, conducted trajectory planning in both joint and Cartesian spaces, and proposed an HPO-GA method for efficient multi-point path planning. The robotic arm demonstrated a 93% success rate in simulated picking, indicating potential for practical tea garden applications.

  • Start: 11.55 AM
  • End: 12.10 AM
  • Location: Houde room 514 (5F), 8th Building, CQU
